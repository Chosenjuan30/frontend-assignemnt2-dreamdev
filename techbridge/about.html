Who we are

About TechBridge Institute

Established in 2009, we have spent over 16 years equipping individuals with transformative digital skills, building a legacy of excellence in technology education across West Africa.

Our story

Pioneering technology education in Nigeria

TechBridge Institute was founded in 2009 by a group of technology professionals and educators who recognised a critical gap: Nigeria and the broader West African region possessed enormous human capital, yet lacked structured, industry-aligned pathways for technology education.

Beginning with a single computer lab and 40 students in Yaba, Lagos, the institute has since grown into a nationally recognised centre of excellence, with campuses in Lagos, Abuja, and Port Harcourt, and a thriving online learning division.

Today, TechBridge is accredited by the National Board for Technical Education (NBTE) and maintains active partnerships with over 60 technology companies, ensuring our programmes remain current, practical, and career-focused.

Our journey

History of TechBridge Institute

From a single room in Yaba to a multi-campus national institution — our milestones tell a story of determination and impact.

2009 — Foundation

Institute established in Lagos

TechBridge Institute was founded by Prof. Afolabi Okonkwo and four co-founders with seed funding of ₦5 million. The inaugural batch of 40 students enrolled in basic computer operations and web design courses held in a rented facility in Yaba, Lagos.

2011 — First accreditation

NBTE accreditation received

The institute received its first formal accreditation from the National Board for Technical Education, legitimising its diploma programmes and enabling graduates to apply for professional certifications.

2013 — Expansion

Abuja campus opens

Driven by demand from the Federal Capital Territory, TechBridge opened its second campus in Garki, Abuja. Enrolment grew to over 1,200 students across both campuses, and the first software development cohort graduated.

2016 — Industry integration

Corporate partnership programme launched

The institute formalised its Corporate Partnership Programme, partnering with 12 technology companies to co-design curricula, provide guest lecturers, and offer guaranteed internships for top-performing graduates.

2018 — Port Harcourt campus

South-south expansion completed

The Port Harcourt campus opened in Trans-Amadi, extending TechBridge's reach to the oil-rich South-South region. A specialised data analytics programme tailored to the energy sector was introduced.

2020 — Digital transformation

Online learning division launched

In response to the COVID-19 pandemic, TechBridge rapidly deployed a full-featured online learning platform. Within six months, over 3,000 students were enrolled in remote programmes, cementing the institute's leadership in digital education delivery.

2022 — Recognition

Named Best Technology Institute in West Africa

TechBridge received the West Africa Technology Education Excellence Award at the annual ECOWAS Education Summit, recognising its graduates' impact across the region's technology sector.

2025 — Present

12,400+ graduates and growing

TechBridge now serves over 2,500 active students, offers 38 accredited programmes, and maintains a 94% graduate employment rate. The institute continues to expand its Women in Tech and Rural Digital Access initiatives.

Our purpose

Mission & vision

M Our mission

To provide high-quality, accessible, and industry-relevant technology education that empowers individuals — regardless of background — to build meaningful careers, contribute to economic development, and drive digital innovation across West Africa.

We achieve this by maintaining rigorous academic standards, fostering strong industry partnerships, and cultivating an inclusive learning environment that celebrates diversity and nurtures every student's full potential.

V Our vision

To be the leading technology education institution in Africa — globally recognised for the quality of our graduates, the depth of our research, and the breadth of our impact on communities and economies across the continent.

By 2030, we aspire to have trained 50,000 technology professionals, launched 500 student-founded ventures, and established TechBridge hubs in every ECOWAS member state.
